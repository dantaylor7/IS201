Inspiration

Feel inspired with powerful words and videos.

The Joy of Easter


Easter brought that impossible news that Christ was raised from the dead. When some of the disciples went to see the sepulchre where Christ was buried they were met by angels who declared, "Why seek ye the living among the dead? He is not here, but is risen." I pray that we may all remember the power that Christ claimed over death in sin during this Easter season.


Liberty Jail


In my opinion, this scene in Liberty Jail is one of the most inspiring moments that anyone can find comfort and strength in. At this moment, Josph Smith was receiving horrible and cruel treatment, his followers were being persecuted and driven from their homes. At this moment he cried to the Savior who responded in one of the most profound responses ever recorded.

"My son, peace be unto thy soul; thine adversity and thine afflictions shall be but a small moment. And then, if thou endure it well, God shall exalt thee on high; thou shalt triumph over all thy foes."

I hope we can find great strength in this message from Jesus Christ. Our efforts and our sufferings do not go unnoticed. Know that you are valued and God is here to support us in all parts of our lives.


The Man in the Arena


This speech, given by President Theodore Roosevelt, is one of my most favorite speeches that I've ever heard. I find great inspiration and motivation by listening to it and thinking about it's message. I hope you can find that same inspiration while viewing it. This speech focuses on our own efforts regardless of whether we fail or triumph. It is a message to everyone that in the end, you are your own critic and the author of your story. We are "those who are actually in the arena" and need to remember that when things get difficult.
